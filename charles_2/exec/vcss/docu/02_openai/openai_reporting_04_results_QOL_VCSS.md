# Results

## Study Population

A total of **30 patients** undergoing radiofrequency ablation for lower limb venous insufficiency were included in the analysis. Outcomes were assessed at baseline (**T0**), early post-operative follow-up (**T1**), and later follow-up (**T2**). VEINES-QOL/Sym data were available for all patients at all timepoints. VCSS was recorded separately for the right and left lower limbs at each timepoint.

---

## VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym Outcomes

### Descriptive Evolution

Mean VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym t-scores improved progressively following intervention. At baseline (T0), mean scores reflected impaired quality of life and symptom burden. Significant increases were observed at T1, with further improvement at T2.

Descriptive statistics for VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym at each timepoint are presented as **mean ± SD**, with corresponding **95% confidence intervals** shown in Figure X.

---

### Model-Based Longitudinal Analysis

In mixed-effects models accounting for repeated measurements within patients, **timepoint was a significant predictor** of both VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym scores. Compared with baseline (T0), estimated mean scores increased significantly at T1 and remained significantly higher at T2.

Model-derived estimated marginal means demonstrated a sustained improvement in patient-reported quality of life and symptom severity over time. Mean changes from baseline with 95% confidence intervals are summarized in Table X.

---

## Venous Clinical Severity Score (VCSS)

### Limb-Level Descriptive Outcomes

VCSS was analysed at the **limb level**, with right and left limbs evaluated separately. At baseline (T0), median VCSS values indicated moderate clinical severity. Following intervention, median VCSS decreased at T1 and showed further reduction at T2, consistent with clinical improvement.

Descriptive VCSS values at each timepoint are reported as **median [interquartile range]** for each limb in Table Y.

---

### Changes from Baseline

Mean changes in VCSS relative to baseline were calculated to quantify clinical improvement. Both limbs demonstrated a reduction in VCSS at T1, with additional improvement observed at T2. The magnitude of change exceeded commonly reported thresholds for clinically meaningful improvement.

Mean changes from baseline with **95% confidence intervals** are presented in Table Z.

---

### Model-Based Analysis of VCSS

A mixed-effects model was used to evaluate longitudinal changes in VCSS while accounting for the hierarchical structure of the data, with repeated observations nested within limbs and limbs nested within patients. Timepoint was included as a fixed effect and patient identifier as a random effect to account for within-patient correlation.

Model-based estimates showed a significant decrease in VCSS over time compared with baseline. The trajectory of improvement was similar for both limbs, with the largest reduction observed at the latest follow-up. Estimated marginal means with 95% confidence intervals are displayed in Figure Y.

---

## Concordance Between Patient-Reported and Clinical Outcomes

Improvements in VEINES-QOL and VEINES-Sym scores were accompanied by parallel reductions in VCSS over time. This concordance between patient-reported outcomes and clinician-assessed clinical severity supports a consistent treatment effect of radiofrequency ablation across both subjective and objective domains.

## 2️⃣ p-value placement: strict consistency rules

This is where many papers fail. Use **one rule everywhere**.

---

### A. Where p-values SHOULD appear

#### ✅ Change / model-based tables only

* Table 3 (VEINES model)
* Table 5 (VCSS change)

**Column example**

```
Mean change vs T0 (95% CI), p-value
```

or

```
p-value vs T0
```

---

#### ✅ Figure captions (optional, minimal)

Only if:

* p < 0.001
* Or global time effect

**Example**

> Overall time effect: p < 0.001.

---

### B. Where p-values should NOT appear

❌ Descriptive tables
❌ Axis labels
❌ Error bars
❌ Raw median/IQR tables

Reviewers strongly prefer this.

---

### C. Which p-values to report

* **Primary**: comparison vs T0
* **Secondary (optional)**: global time effect from mixed model

Avoid:

* T1 vs T2 unless clinically justified

---

### D. Recommended wording (copy-safe)

* “Compared with baseline (T0), …”
* “Timepoint was significantly associated with …”

Avoid:

* “proved”
* “caused”
* “dramatic improvement”
